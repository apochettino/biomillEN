Our genetically modified animals (GMA) platform.

At Biosidus we have deveeloped an expression system in macroorganisms (transgenic bovines) only requiring milking to obtain the active pharmaceutical ingredient (human protein) in their milk.

The project consists in the generation of a transgenic bovine by incorporating some genetic information in its DNA intended to produce a human protein (or target protein of recombinant origin), expressed in its milk under the control of the beta casein promoter. This enabled the generation of a herd of animals capable of consistently producing a human protein secreted to the transgenic cow’s milk at industrial amounts. The milk containing the human protein is further processed at the downstream laboratories undergoing a tandem of different isolation and purification steps to become the active pharmaceutical ingredient (API) suitable for the manufacture of pharmaceutical products for human or veterinary use, as well as food additives.





One of the advantages of the transgenic animal system is to replace large upstream areas and cell fermentation equipment by dairy cattle. This results in substantial savings in upfront investments and operational costs. Direct costs for this platform are particularly reduced since cattle consumables are water, corn and lucerne/alfalfa, and labor force is reduced to milking workforce instead of professional biotechnologists or technicians. This productive system is also more flexible since production (number of productive cattle) can be scale up according to market demand.





LFinally, it is possible to identify a positive impact on environment as the productive “leftover” is just milk, of low ecological impact, different from the traditional fermentation procedures which need to be degraded / inactivated before withdrawal. Therefore, the genetic modified animal platform can be recognized as more advantageous technology from the environmental viewpoint compared to the traditional fermentation systems. This transgenic animal-based production technology has been globally recognized and patents covering the relevant IP rights have been granted in different countries.
