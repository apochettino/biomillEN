VHH

VHH constitutes a family of heavy chain nanoantibodies which combine the advantages of both monoclonal antibodies and small molecule drugs. They are used in gene therapy and as anti-infectious agents, in intracellular studies, diagnostic kits, among other uses. We have focused on VHH use against rotavirus, endemic infantile disease in our country. Rotavirus (RV) is the leading cause of severe diarrhea among children worldwide. RV gastroenteritis is characterized by a profuse watery diarrhea which may lead to severe dehydration and eventually death. Globally, RV accounts for 25-50% of infantile gastroenteritis requiring hospitalization in developed and underdeveloped countries, respectively. Each year, this infectious agent is responsible for 4 million clinic visits, 2.4 million hospitalizations and 500,000 deaths of children under 5 years old.

As a result of a development conducted in collaboration with INTA Argentina -National Institute of Agricultural Technology, VHH producing genes were obtained from llamas -South American camelid, characterized by their specificity, resistance, and viral neutralization capacity. Said genes were used to design an expression cassette which was implanted in bovines which became capable of expressing VHH in their milk. Nanoantibodies present in milk have a protective function against RV. Therefore, this milk may turn into functional food or a source of nanoantibodies as active ingredient of pharmaceutical formulations or nutritional additives.
